ABOUT

HI, I'M LOGAN BRANDWOOD,
A 19 YEAR OLD GRAPHIC DESIGN STUDENT LIVING IN OTTAWA.

I am passionate about design, illustration and typography. I love to work with creative people and I live to make the world a prettier place. Since a young age, I have been interested in design. I was a yearbook editor, technitian and photographer for 3 years of highschool, and I have recently finished my first year of college. I have found that fulfilling my passion as a career is one of the most amazing experiences possible. I get to do the very thing that thrives and entices me on a daily basis.

I am currently looking for a position as graphic designer, and I am available for freelance work. Don't forget to check out my portfolio and feel free to contact me.

PORTFOLIO

Pablo Lobato Recreation

Illustrator

These two pieces of Jeffree Star and Victoria Beckham were one of my favourite and most enjoyable projects to date. Recreating Lobato's style was both challenging and educational through the use of Gestalt, repetition and colour theory

iPhone Skin

InDesign

Open concept projects allow me to use all aspects of my design abilities. Learning and creating are two things that go hand in hand when designing a piece like this, such as creating roses from scratch and using clipping paths. This is an all time favourite.

Theatre Poster

Photoshop, InDesign

This is an advertising poster for a script-reading event held at Algonquin college that had to incorporate all genres of acting. This poster uses the left-brain, right- brain analogy, based on a writers creativity. It was chosen out of all other entries.

Dust Jacket Redesign

Photoshop, InDesign

For this piece, I aimed at creating a very modern, edgy book cover. I have always been interested in learning double exposure photography but have not yet had the chance, so I decided to do it on my own using photoshop.

Graphite/Coloured Pencil

The challenge of using coloured pencil alongside graphite pencils is not to mix them together or else everything will become muddy! This is one of my proudest pieces from my first semester of school.
